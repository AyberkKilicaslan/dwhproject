Data Preprocessing

Before applying models for player position prediction, the dataset needs to be pre-processed. Firstly, the variables that were unnecessary to be used in the classification model were excluded from the data set. Then, the categorical variables found in the original data were encoded. At this stage, the sub-region positions of the four main positions of the defender, midfielder, striker and goalkeeper positions have been returned to the main positions. At the same time, the goalkeeper position was deciphered among the prediction results because the abilities that goalkeepers have are different from those of players in the other position. After checking the missing value, all values of each feature are checked, their outliers on different positions were cleared. Thus, the large effect of outliers on this variable was purified. With the help of correlation map, linear relationships between the three main locations were checked and the variables that were considered to have little effect on the classification result were deciphered from the table. While applying this process, it was decided to remove variables whose correlation value was not above +40 and not below -40 in three different comparisons of the three positions. Feature scaling and normalization operations were not applied because the variables other than the single categorical variable in the data series are numeric and are limited to 0- 100. Separately from the model that was trained in its original state, the versions that were transformed in a logarithmic base of 10 were also trained and the results were compared with each other. In this comparison, the two results came out very close to each other.
